## Wording and style rules learned

- Prefer broad, durable animal facts over one-off oddities.
- Keep stems short and concrete, usually one idea each.
- Favor behavior, adaptation, classification, and ecosystem-role questions.
- Use plausible same-domain distractors, not joke answers.
- Keep survival and foraging content conservative and safety-aware.
- Prefer stable biome/process questions over fast-changing environmental metrics.
- Prefer practical outdoor judgment questions where a wrong answer would teach a bad habit.
- Keep answers short and concrete even when the topic is technical.

## Weak patterns to avoid

- overly obvious animal-superlative gimmes without context
- sensational predator framing
- unstable population or conservation-number questions
- source-name phrasing in stems or explanations
- advice that implies wild foods or plants are safe without clear identification
- overusing bare superlatives instead of meaningful ecological facts
- dramatic or myth-driven survival advice
- answer choices that make the correct option obvious by tone or realism
