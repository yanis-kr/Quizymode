## Wording and style rules learned

- Use plain language even when the topic is abstract.
- Favor significance, context, and interpretive value over bare name recall.
- Keep questions compact and tie them to works, movements, ideas, or institutions.
- Let explanations briefly show why the answer matters.
- Prefer stable cultural facts over trend-driven media trivia.

## Weak patterns to avoid

- vague theory-speak
- quote-fragment recall without meaning
- listy name drills with no context
- pretentious phrasing
- distractors that are absurd or outside the topic domain
