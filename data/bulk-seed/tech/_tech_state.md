## Wording and style rules learned

- Use simple technical English even for advanced topics.
- Favor component roles, tradeoffs, and practical meaning over memorization.
- Prefer durable concepts that survive version churn.
- Keep distractors in the same technical domain and similar level of specificity.
- Use explanations to clarify the concept directly, not restate marketing language.

## Weak patterns to avoid

- stale buzzword prompts
- version-specific hype or release notes disguised as knowledge
- syntax trivia without conceptual value
- vendor claims that are not stable across time
- absurd distractors that make the answer obvious
